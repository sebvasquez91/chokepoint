The engine view

How this was made

one article in, one playable experience out — stage by stage

CHOKEPOINT was generated from a single piece of journalism — “I Saw the Face of God in a Semiconductor Factory” (Virginia Heffernan, WIRED, 2023) — through the documented pipeline below. For this demonstrator the stages were run with a human editor guiding an AI system; the R&D programme behind it automates each stage inside PressPlay, Kita’s engine for turning journalism into playable experiences, with the journalist kept in editorial control.

STAGE 01

Source capture

The article is captured with paragraph-level addressing — 108 paragraphs, each with a stable ID (P001–P108). Those IDs become the provenance backbone: every downstream artefact, and every ¶ chip in the game, points back to them.

In the engine

Ingestion from CMS, URL or file; rights metadata; the publisher’s own content, on the publisher’s own terms.

Artefact: 01-article.public.md (paragraph map; full text stays local out of respect for WIRED’s copyright)

STAGE 02

Story model — what does the article actually assert?

Schema-constrained extraction of the article’s entities, quantities, quotes, causal claims and theses — where every field must cite a paragraph span. Ungrounded output is rejected, not guessed: the no-invented-facts rule is enforced at the data layer, before any game exists.

ExtractedExample (with source)
50 claims“~92% of the world’s most advanced chips” → P002
6 theses“Manufactured indispensability” → P060, P068
10 causal edgespure-play → trust → crux position → shield → P070, P015
5 sensitivity flagsconflict framing, viewpoint, corrections (P106)…

STAGE 03

Format selection — what kind of play fits this journalism?

The story model is scored against a taxonomy of interaction patterns. The binding rule: a format that would need facts the article doesn’t contain scores low, however tempting the topic. This article’s causal meat is historical-strategic, so an operational logistics sim (the obvious guess from the headline) lost to:

FormatScore
Role-taking decision simulation across historical eras5/5 — chosen
Dependency-network “break the chain” (epilogue)4/5 — chosen as coda
Timeline reconstruction3/5
Operational logistics sim2/5 — would require external facts

Artefact: 03-format-selection.md — including the honest record of the human’s wrong first guess

▼

STAGE 04

Mechanic spec — the game, parameterised by the article

The chosen template (era-strategy) is instantiated: six decision nodes from the article’s own arc (umbrellas → abandonment → apprenticeship → pure-play → the litho crisis → Arizona), four meters named in the article’s value language, and a scoring rule with a designed property: the historically true path is the optimal strategy — mastering the game means comprehending the article.

Artefact: 04-mechanic-spec.md

▼

STAGE 05

Constrained generation — content as data

All era briefs, options, outcomes and receipts are generated into a single data file, content.js — separate from the reusable game runtime (app.js). That split is the product: the runtime is the engine’s mechanic template; the data file is what gets generated per article. A different article, same template, different game.

Artefact: 05-content-provenance.md — every in-game element mapped to its source span

▼

STAGE 06

Integrity check — journalism’s non-negotiables

A distortion review against the source: could the game imply anything the article doesn’t support? Counterfactual choices get visible MODELLED badges; conflict is discussed only in sourced quotes, never simulated; the article’s published correction is honoured; even a source-internal inconsistency (the article calls Savary “Jacques” in one paragraph and “Thomas” in another) is flagged rather than silently resolved.

Artefact: 06-fidelity-check.md
