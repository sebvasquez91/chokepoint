# Pipeline stage 08 — Narrative design (RPG format)

**What this stage is**: the second format generated from the same story model. After playtesting format A (era-strategy),
the commissioning editor (Seb) issued a format override: *"a 2D narrative RPG with relevant characters, dialogues,
dynamics and quests."* In the engine, this is a supported editorial input — format selection ranks options, but the
editor picks. This document is the full narrative design: story arc, characters, quests, systems, scenes, dialogue
principles, and the integrity rules specific to dramatising real reported people.

**Design thesis**: the article is *already* a quest narrative. It is a first-person pilgrimage — a journalist trying to
get inside the most restricted building on Earth, refused three times "like a fairy tale" (P024), earning access through
understanding, and being transformed by what she finally beholds (P090–P101). The RPG doesn't impose game structure on
the journalism; it surfaces the game structure the journalism already has. Fidelity and drama align.

---

## 1 · Premise, protagonist, stakes

- **You play a WIRED journalist** retracing the pilgrimage the article reports (protagonist unnamed; the About panel
  discloses that the player character follows the author's reported journey). No WIRED journalist has ever toured a
  TSMC fab (P018). Your assignment: get inside — and understand what the place *means*.
- **The want**: see the fabs. **The need**: comprehend the island that built them. The game's gate makes the need the
  route to the want: **understanding is the key that opens the fab** — which is also the engine's whole thesis.
- **Stakes** are the article's stakes: democracy's anxious decade (P001, P008), an unnamed threat across 110 miles of
  water (P004 — see integrity rule 7), and the strange hope that interdependence can hold the world together (P013, P102).
- **Tone**: wry, literary, awed. The narrator's registers — jet-lagged irony (mall clothes, Burger King), intellectual
  play (Savary, Blake, Frankenstein), and unguarded reverence at the end.

## 2 · Story arc (three acts + prologue)

### Prologue — New York, the night before *(3 min)*
Small apartment, night palette. Interactive beats: pack the Samsonite (Chris Miller's *Chip War* and Hirschman's *The
Passions and the Interests* go in — planting the loss, P009); a phone call with **Keith Krach** (pre-departure, exactly
as the article frames it, P017): the porcupine idea seeded. On the desk, a podcast player: **Adam Tooze** — "us up
against the face of God, in a sense" (P038) → title card, main quest granted: **THE SACRED MOUNTAIN**. Exit → smash cut:
"18 hallucinatory hours at the back of a packed 777" (P008).

### Act 1 — Arrival *(8 min)* — Taoyuan baggage claim → Taipei street
- **Baggage claim**: the carousel empties. Clerk gives the whole explanation the article got: **"Ukraine war."** (P009)
  → side quest **TWO WORDS AT BAGGAGE CLAIM** (open until Act 2's end — the article resolves it only when the tour is
  granted, P057). Your books are in the lost bag; inner monologue establishes the pilgrim frame (P001).
- **Taipei street, dusk**: hotel check-in; the **third-storey mini-mall open after hours** — toothbrush and "shapeless
  navy-blue separates" (P020; outfit change, sprite swap) → quest **SPYCORE** (P055: "surely mall clothes make for
  superb spycore"); the mall clerk teaches **chabuduo** — "something like *whatever*. Or *close enough*" (P020) →
  unlocks a recurring zen dialogue option across the whole game.
- **7-Eleven — first meeting with Michael Kramer** over black coffee he buys with the company discount (P036, P023).
  The ask, and **REFUSAL №1** (P024). Kramer, teasing but fair, sets the pilgrimage: *learn the island; learn the
  litho; learn the stakes* — the three pillars (a diegetic rendering of the article's three braided threads). Act 2 unlocked.
- **Newsstand**: papers euphemise the threat as "cross-strait tensions" (P004) → notebook entry; the asteroid line as
  monologue. Optional: TV-shop window runs a **Robert Tsao** segment (see character roster).

### Act 2 — The pilgrimage *(20–25 min, three pillars, any order)*

**Pillar I · THE ISLAND (history)** — Starbucks with **Victor Chan**, historian, over "mediocre flatbread" (P058).
Chan talks in a steady stream — and walks you *into* the memories (sepia flashback vignettes, playable):
- **V1 · The umbrella line (1970s)**: work a beat on an umbrella line; foreman states the fact like a badge — three of
  every four umbrellas on Earth (P059).
- **V2 · The gates of Taishan**: Mattel's notice; Barbie leaves for cheaper labour; Nixon context; the town devastated
  (P060). The vow, full-screen text card: **"Invaluable, rather, so it couldn't be neglected or pushed around."** (P060)
- **V3 · The apprenticeship**: RCA classroom, 1976 (P061 — including, preserved with the author's irony, who staffed
  the early fabs and why, P061); the education money flowing while the Cultural Revolution burned expertise across the
  strait (P063); An Wang's koan — "How do you make a computer?" (P064–P065); K. T. Li persuading **Morris Chang** —
  rendered as a silhouetted tableau with the Song-poem line about climbing the tower to survey all roads (P066).
- Debrief with Chan: "Chips that come with ketchup? We had no clue." (P062); chips replace umbrellas and Barbies (P068).

**Pillar II · THE LITHO (science)** — bullet train to Hsinchu (transition scene, strait thoughts).
- **TSMC HQ**: doormen sternly request no photographs of a building that looks like "a '90s convention center in…
  Charlotte, North Carolina" (P022); normcore lobby; Kramer's Burger King reveal — "Ten percent." (P023).
- **Mark Liu, the chairman**, in the trophy conference room (Yamaha treasure ship; the Lego model of the FinFET, P027):
  "We are doing atomic constructions… Think like an atomic-sized person" (P027); curiosity and stamina (P026); "Every
  scientist must believe in God" (P026); ions into silicon at Berkeley (P028 — notebook records the article's published
  correction, P106); "You always feel you are scratching the surface. Until, one day, it's revealed to you." (P028);
  and his discomfort with the Sacred Mountain label — "That label makes us a sore thumb." (P019)
- **Burn-Jeng Lin** in a university room (P087): the ~2000 crisis staged as a playable flashback choice — the
  calcium-fluoride lens (a reported billion-dollar dead end, P081), waiting for EUV (10 reliable watts, P081), or
  shooting the light **through water** (P082). "Water is a miracle… God is kind to the fish. And also to us." (P082);
  "I see God in any scale" (P083); "Well, we just have to solve them. That is the TSMC spirit." (P088). He still says
  "us" (P089) — and he points you to his successor.
- **Anthony Yen** (now head of research at ASML, P084), by phone from the hotel, opening the game's best set-piece:
  **the night of 14 October 2014** — playable flashback in the dark fab. You gown up and take the power-source dial:
  10 watts… 90. The eureka (P085). Yen runs out "flinging off his bunny suit — 'I was euphoric. I was on drugs. For the
  believer, it is quite a religious experience.'" (P086). Today: just over 2 nanometres, production 2025 (P086); some
  56,000 patents (P075).

**Pillar III · THE STAKES (trust & geopolitics)** — mostly from the hotel and the street.
- **Krach, the follow-up call**: the porcupine in full (P017/P069); the masked 30-country Clean Network tour and
  Huawei routed (P048–P049); ~15% of chips still from China, a trillion-dollar czar (P049); the CHIPS Act grown from
  his $12bn deal to ~$280bn (P044); "Xi is absolutely obsessed with the semiconductor business" (P044); "Trust in
  technology is everything" (P046); the trust list (P051); and his name for Chang: "the oracle" (P071).
- **Kramer, coffee №2 — the business model**: pure play as trust — "TSMC stealing from chip designers would be like a
  printing press stealing plots from novelists" (P070); Midway and Stalingrad (P070); "TSMC gets 92 percent" (P070);
  Jensen Huang's joke on a lobby poster — "Basically, there is air—and TSMC" (P016); indispensable-but-invisible (P074).
- **TV-shop window**: Gina Raimondo at the Phoenix unveiling — "a national security vulnerability… Today, we say we're
  changing that" (P052); ten thousand jobs, Tim Cook, "American-made chips" (P053) — which will still be
  Taiwanese-engineered "up to the femtosecond" from Hsinchu (P054); entente, not independence (P053). On the next
  channel: Tsao in rococo tactical gear (P011).

**The refusals cadence** (the fairy-tale gate, P024): №1 at first ask; **№2** after your first pillar (Kramer, warmer:
"Twice more, and you're a knight. Or a daughter of King Lear." — the article's own image, P024); **№3** after the
second pillar (he stops teasing; asks the sincerity question). Completing all three pillars + the honesty beat ("Why do
you want in?" — the beholding answer: "To observe and to behold are two different pastures", P091) flips the gate:
Kramer requests your measurements for a bunny suit and shoe protectors (P057). **That evening**: the tour lands on the
calendar; your Samsonite arrives (P057 — quest resolved, books recovered); the COVID-test night — two hallucinated red
lines, then clear (P076); N95s laid out.

### Act 3 — The Face of God *(8–10 min, the transformation)*
Fab 12A — "a GigaFab because, every month, it processes fully 100,000 of the biggest wafers" (P057).
- **Antechamber rituals**, each a small interaction: the turnstile ("brings to mind *The Phantom Tollbooth*", P092);
  the single machine that washes, rinses and dries your hands (P092); the Roman-bath antechamber; the orderlies fitting
  gown and booties — the tender beat, played slow: "Our bodies are not quite here." (P093). Air: up to 1,000,000 dust
  particles per m³ outside; **no more than 100** in here (P094).
- **The hall**: palette shifts to white-gold luminosity; the HUD fades; movement slows to a walk. Waypoints, not
  objectives: the featureless white machines (P099); the lithography machine — your eyes mist, and emotions are
  "shameful contaminants" (P097, with the Laocoön image); then the epiphany the whole game has been walking toward —
  **the machines resemble incubators in a neonatal ICU** (P100): "tiny souls… utterly dependent on adults who cherish
  them… To see the fabs is to feel a full-body urge to keep the tiny marvelous creations—newborns—and then humanity as
  a whole—alive." (P101). Blake's grain of sand callback (P087).
- **Exit**: the clock — an hour inside, felt like twenty minutes; "time flies in the fabs" (P098).
- **Farewells**: Liu — "I hope the bad guys will get their penalty… And I hope the righteous human collaboration will
  continue." (P103); "God made silicon for us." (P104). Kramer — one last tease (his laughing at your obsession, P090).
  Street at dusk, closing monologue: the Enlightenment credo (P102) and "a universe inside our universe… enlightened
  with ultraviolet and built on sand." (P105)
- **Epilogue screens**: notebook recap (insights & receipts counts, per pillar); "What was real?" panel (the integrity
  conventions, with counts); read-the-original CTA; credits & attribution; links to the pipeline view and to format A.

**Progression economy**: insights land in the **notebook** (three pillars: ISLAND / LITHO / STAKES; ~26 entries, every
one carrying ¶ receipts). Kramer's final gate checks pillar completeness — the game literally will not open the fab to
a player who hasn't understood the journalism. Sincerity is a light social meter (honest/curious choices warm Kramer's
lines; cynical ones sharpen the teasing — flavour, never a fail state). **Chabuduo** is a recurring optional response
that de-dramatises moments (lost luggage, the COVID scare) — the article's own coping meme as a dialogue mechanic (P020, P056).

## 6 · Dialogue principles (+ samples)

1. Verbatim article quotes render with a **¶ chip inline**; tapping opens the receipt sheet (quote, ¶ ref, attribution, link).
2. Non-verbatim lines are connective tissue **in character**, asserting only facts/beliefs/actions the article attributes
   to that person. If the article doesn't support a line, the line doesn't exist.
3. The player's monologue may quote the article's narration (tagged ¶). The best lines in this game were written by
   Virginia Heffernan; the game's job is to earn them their moment.
4. Choices are questions, tones, and the occasional chabuduo — never trivia checks. Wrong options don't exist; shallow
   ones get shallower answers (dynamics, not punishment).

**Sample — Refusal №1 (7-Eleven).**
> KRAMER: The coffee's good here. That's not company loyalty — the discount is ten percent. ¶
> YOU: (choice) ① "I want to see inside a fab." ② "Why does everyone call it the Sacred Mountain?" ③ [chabuduo] "Nice coffee."
> — on ①: KRAMER: No. *(beat)* You're the — fourth? — this year. They want the machines. The machines don't photograph.
> What would you even be looking at? · YOU: I don't want to look. I want to behold. · KRAMER: *(laughs)* …Learn the island.
> Learn the litho. Learn the stakes. Then ask me again.

**Sample — Lin, the water line.**
> LIN: Everyone wanted a braver light. A lens grown like a diamond. Furnaces — hundreds. ¶ *(receipt: P081)*
> LIN: I asked a smaller question. What refracts, and behaves, and is *already here*?
> LIN: “Water is a miracle. Not only for TSMC. It's a miracle for the whole of mankind. God is kind to the fish. And also to us.” ¶ *(P082)*

## 7 · Integrity rules v2 — dramatising real people (extends stage 06)

1. **Verbatim tagging** in the dialogue UI (¶ chips); everything else is paraphrase under rule 2.
2. **No invented positions**: paraphrase may only carry what the article attributes (statements, beliefs, biography, actions).
3. **Characterisation from the author's own descriptions** (Kramer's slept-in style; Lin's Gene Kelly; Liu's guileless wonder) — quoted in the receipts.
4. **Interview framing mirrors the reporting**: Krach by phone weeks before departure (P017); Chang never appears in the present (he isn't interviewed in the piece) — flashback only; Tsao's absence *is* the reported fact (P011).
5. **The unnamed-threat convention is kept as a formal device**: like the article (P004), the game never names the threat in narration; it exists in euphemism, weather, and the shield logic. No conflict is simulated (carried from stage 06).
6. **The protagonist is disclosed** as a dramatised stand-in retracing the author's reported journey; her monologue quotes are tagged.
7. **Corrections honoured** (P106 → Liu's Berkeley doctorate in the notebook); the Jacques/Thomas Savary inconsistency remains flagged, not fixed.
8. **About panel** carries all of the above in plain language, with counts (verbatim lines vs authored lines).

## 8 · Production spec (engine targets)

Self-contained canvas RPG, no frameworks: procedural 16px tile set ×3 scale, authored pixel-matrix sprites (2-frame walk,
4 facings) and 24px portraits; DOM dialogue layer (typewriter, portraits, choices, ¶ chips); quest log / notebook /
inventory panels; scripted cutscenes (walks, palette fades, chapter cards); flashback palette swap; touch D-pad + A
button; keyboard + screen-reader-friendly DOM text; localStorage autosave; WebAudio ambient pads + interaction blips
(muted toggle, gesture-gated). Format A remains playable at `/strategy.html`; the pipeline page gains a "one story
model, two formats" section. Playtime target: 25–40 minutes.
